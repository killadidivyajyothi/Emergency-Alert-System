 Emergency-Alert-System
 An Emergency Alert System (EAS) is a national or regional public warning system designed to quickly and effectively disseminate urgent alerts and updates during emergencies. It allows authorized government officials and agencies to broadcast critical messages across multiple communication channels such as television, radio, mobile phones (via Wireless Emergency Alerts), sirens, emails, social media, and apps. The purpose is to provide timely, accurate, and actionable information to the public to prevent harm and enable quick response during crises like natural disasters (hurricanes, tornadoes, floods), public safety threats (terrorism, bomb threats), public health emergencies, missing persons alerts (Amber Alerts), and major infrastructure failures.
The system uses technology such as Specific Area Message Encoding (SAME) to target alerts to specific geographical areas so only the affected population receives the message. Modern EAS integrates various digital tools and a coordinated network of alert originators, dissemination platforms, content delivery networks, and monitoring systems to ensure reliable and widespread alert delivery. It significantly improves preparedness and public safety by enabling rapid response and clear communication when time is critical.
In addition to large-scale systems, there are projects developed for specific platforms like smartphones or IoT-enabled emergency alert systems that can detect and notify authorities about incidents like vehicle accidents, fires, gas leaks, etc. These projects often use technologies like GPS for precise location tracking and GSM/IoT for immediate communication to emergency services and concerned contacts.
